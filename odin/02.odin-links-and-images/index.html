The rel="noopener noreferrer" is used for security reasons.

  • "noopener" means the opened link will not have access to change the page (this is known as tabnabbing, and most recent browsers are secured against this by default, as they always include "noopener" implicitly when target="_blank" is used).
  • "noreferrer" means the opened link will not know which is the Referrer, i.e., which website was the previous.
